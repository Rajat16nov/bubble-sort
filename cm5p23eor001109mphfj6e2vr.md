### Identify the differences between regulatory, educational, and promotional medical writing

Regulatory medical writing involves creating documents for regulatory agencies for approvals of new drugs or devices.

It follows strict guidelines and focuses on safety and efficacy data.

Educational medical writing is aimed at healthcare professionals or patients, providing information to improve understanding and decision-making.

It focuses on disease understanding, treatment options, etc.

Promotional medical writing is often aimed at marketing a particular drug or treatment, meant to persuade potential customers, but it must also be compliant with regulations about false claims.

<strong>ChatGPT Prompt:</strong>

```plaintext
As a seasoned medical writer, identify the differences between regulatory, educational, and promotional medical writing.

Consider factors such as their main purposes, target audience, and the kind of information they typically present.
```
