### 🧬 AI Mined 400K Reddit Posts to Find Drug Side Effects That Clinical Trials Missed

- Penn researchers fed [**over 400,000 Reddit posts**](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.dSnm3kaGd0BkNqLYPjeMf9Enll956Jh63GaFW_ziZ7vGiidUBWj7yPBP7PXRNYeFxz6lhoaOVpRWrcQLGPw_Wn-w9AO-Wsm2GudIN9Yc6VHJ-PUF-ebO7ROtN9b729b1uOxqNVLddBmXxel4G2DLb5YlMWoD8G6XI0KblmK6NjM7yvUCIVrYSpAPI6qOqYHPl6VLqC-8jt5fqYmFjv7-uM369kb0H6Hp47nNsB9DBkiXOEdCzZytrwpRRKETXuIWdPyc7pakQZ8iFqltZwoIyMJK0hUeC5_PaOmxlUKtY4g/4pr/JXXInq2WSg6SokNKidPRFg/h20/h001.vJ7_gX1-8delkVpuXhihKGwrQ8D8LjS0FuN8orz2YDA?utm_source=galaxy.ai&utm_medium=newsletter) about **Ozempic and Mounjaro** into GPT and Gemini using a technique called "computational social listening," mapping user reports to standardized medical terms across 5+ years of discussions. Nearly **half the sample** reported at least one side effect - flagging **menstrual irregularities, chills, hot flashes, and fatigue** that *aren't on current drug labels* and barely showed up in clinical trials. Researcher Lyle Ungar compared Reddit to a "neighborhood grapevine" of real-time patient notes.

- **Why it matters:** FINALLYYYY an LLM use case that's genuinely useful. Reddit isn't a peer-reviewed journal, but when **thousands of people flag the same symptoms**, that signal is hard to ignore. AI just made it possible to listen to patients at scale - and pharma companies should be paying attention.
