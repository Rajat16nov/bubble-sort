### Understand the difference between summarizing, paraphrasing and quoting

Summarizing, paraphrasing, and quoting are all strategies used in writing to include someone else's ideas.

However, they are used differently.

Summarizing involves condensing the main idea of a large piece of text into a concise overview.

Paraphrasing is the process of restating someone else's ideas in your own words while maintaining the original meaning.

Quoting, on the other hand, is using the exact words from the source and attributing it to the original author.

<strong>ChatGPT Prompt:</strong>

```plaintext
As an experienced writer, illustrate the differences between summarizing, paraphrasing and quoting.

Take a passage from a provided text and demonstrate how each technique would be applied.

Here is the passage to be used:
```
