## ChatGPT Prompts for Nonfiction Writing

### Identify a compelling nonfiction topic

With the vast array of themes available in nonfiction, it can be challenging to identify a compelling topic.

But don't worry, you can use ChatGPT to help you with that.

Tell ChatGPT about the kind of audience you're targeting, the issues they're interested in, or the overall theme you want to explore.

It will then suggest a series of compelling nonfiction topics for you to consider.

<strong>ChatGPT Prompt:</strong>

```plaintext
Act as an experienced nonfiction writer and suggest compelling topics for a book.

The audience consists of millennials who are interested in environmental issues and sustainable living.
```

### Conduct research on the chosen topic

To enhance your nonfiction writing, you need to conduct thorough research on your chosen topic.

ChatGPT can be a powerful tool in this process.

Just provide ChatGPT with the subject you are writing about, and it can provide information, context, and viewpoints related to it.

For instance, you can ask ChatGPT to provide historical context, present statistics, or explain complex concepts in your research.

<strong>ChatGPT Prompt:</strong>

```plaintext
Assist as an expert in nonfiction writing to conduct in-depth research.

I am writing a nonfiction book about the impact of climate change on coastal cities.

Provide me with key historical events, current statistics, and relevant theories about this topic.
```

### Add citations and references

When writing nonfiction, it's crucial to back up your points with valid sources.

ChatGPT can assist in adding appropriate citations and references to bolster your credibility.

You can provide the information or data that you want to cite, and ChatGPT can format it according to the preferred citation style (APA, MLA, Chicago, etc.).

<strong>ChatGPT Prompt:</strong>

```plaintext
Act as a proficient academic writer and provide suitable citations and references for these information pieces:

1.

A study on climate change published in 2021 in the journal Nature.

2.

The book 1984 by George Orwell, published by Penguin Random House.

3.

An article titled The Future of AI from the website TechCrunch dated March 2022.

Please use the APA citation style.
```
