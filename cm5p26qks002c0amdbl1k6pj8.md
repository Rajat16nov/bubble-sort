### Understand the pattern day trader (PDT) rule

The Pattern Day Trader (PDT) rule is a regulation enforced by the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA) in the US.

It states that any trader who executes four or more day trades within five business days, provided that the number of day trades is more than six percent of the trader's total trades in the margin account for that same five business day period, is considered a pattern day trader.

As a PDT, you must maintain a minimum equity of $25,000 in your trading account at all times.

<strong>ChatGPT Prompt:</strong>

```plaintext
As a financial advisor experienced in day trading, explain the implications of the Pattern Day Trader (PDT) rule.

Who does it impact and what are its requirements?
```
