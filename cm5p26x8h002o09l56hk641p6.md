### Explain the concept of compound interest

Compound interest is a financial concept where the interest earned on an amount of money is added back to the original amount, increasing the base for future interest calculations.

In terms of budgeting, it plays a key role in growing savings and investments over time, making it a powerful tool for wealth generation.

For example, if you have $1000 in a savings account with a yearly interest rate of 5%, after one year, the interest will be added to the original amount, making it $1050, which becomes the new base for interest calculation in the next year.

<strong>ChatGPT Prompt:</strong>

```plaintext
Act as a financial advisor to explain the concept of compound interest and how it affects budgeting and long-term financial planning.

Illustrate with an example of a savings account with a certain amount and an annual interest rate.
```
