### Learn the difference between call and put options

In options trading, understanding the difference between call and put options is crucial.

A call option gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy the underlying asset at a predetermined price before the contract expires.

On the other hand, a put option gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to sell the underlying asset at a fixed price before the contract's expiration.

<strong>ChatGPT Prompt:</strong>

```plaintext

```

### Understand the concept of 'in the money' and 'out of the money' options

In options trading, 'in the money' (ITM) and 'out of the money' (OTM) are terms that help traders gauge the value and potential profitability of an option.

An option is 'in the money' when the exercise price is favorable compared to the market price of the underlying asset.

In contrast, an option is 'out of the money' when the exercise price is not favorable compared to the market price.
